I'm not sure if it's me or if anyone else ever experiences that initial awkwardness when writing about yourself. You stop and think, "I need to convince these people that I’m awesome because I am. Well, don’t get too confident. But also don’t mistake humility with tearing yourself down." And that is how I landed here for my introduction. When thinking about coding, I realize it is a very human thing. It's algorithms and one zeros and markup and markdown sure. But what we build is to be used by people. So we must be okay with being human, with admitting there is more to learn and others to learn from. I myself am a graduate of a liberal arts college, Spring Arbor University to be exact. There, after 5 years (needed a "victory" lap), I received my degree in English. I also received and took to heart part of our alma mater, to be a lifelong learner. That is how ended up in a coding bootcamp (We Can Code It) learning full stack development in ASP.NET. I have some snapshots and the links for the code in my projects. Feel free to go and check them out. Also check out my blog about my journey through game development and coding and general ways life influences my coding and vice-versa.
